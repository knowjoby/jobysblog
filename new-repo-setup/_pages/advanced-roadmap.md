*For PMs with 1–2 years of AI product experience who want to go deeper.*

---

## Stage 1: Master AI Product Strategy (Months 1–2)

**Goal:** Move from shipping features to shaping AI product direction.

- Define where AI creates defensible value (data flywheels, network effects, personalization moats)
- Identify the right AI approach: rule-based, classical ML, or generative AI
- Build a multi-quarter AI product roadmap aligned to business outcomes
- Learn to evaluate build vs. buy vs. fine-tune decisions

---

## Stage 2: Go Deep on LLMs and Generative AI (Months 2–3)

**Goal:** Become the go-to PM for LLM-powered products.

- Understand how large language models work at a product level (context windows, temperature, RAG)
- Learn prompt engineering basics — know what's possible, what's fragile
- Evaluate LLM APIs: OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, open-source alternatives
- Design evals: how do you measure if your LLM feature is actually working?

---

## Stage 3: Responsible AI in Practice (Months 3–4)

**Goal:** Ship AI that users can trust.

- Identify bias risks in your specific use case
- Build feedback mechanisms so users can flag bad AI outputs
- Write an AI feature card (model card for PMs): limitations, confidence levels, known failure modes
- Engage with legal/compliance early on data usage and model outputs

---

## Stage 4: Advanced Experimentation (Months 4–6)

**Goal:** Run rigorous tests on AI features.

- Design A/B tests where the treatment is a model change (not just UI)
- Handle the "novelty effect" in AI feature rollouts
- Set up interleaving experiments for ranking/recommendation systems
- Build dashboards that track model drift alongside product metrics

---

## Key Skills to Develop
- SQL for querying model outputs and user behavior logs
- Basic Python to read notebooks and prototype prompts
- Stakeholder communication: translating model uncertainty to leadership
